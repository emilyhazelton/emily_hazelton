The Data Set

This report explores loan data provided by the online lender Prosper.The data spans from November, 2005 to March, 2014, with 113,937 observations.

The data used in this exploration was tidied and provided by Udacity for the EDA learning section of their online Data Analysis degree. The original set included 81 variables, and my instructions were to evaluate 10-15 variables for my EDA. I chose the following variables:

  • Loan amount
  • Loan term months
  • Loan status current
  • Employment status
  • Credit score lower
  • Credit score upper
  • Debt income ratio
  • Stated income monthly
  • Principal paid
  • Interest and fees paid
  • Estimated return

I follow a standard EDA process in this project, progressing from univariate to bivariate to multivariate explorations. In the summary section, I pick three visualizations from the EDA. These graphs are what I would communicate to stakeholders to share what I learned during my exploration.

Loan amounts cluster vary, with many loans equal to or less than $10,000. The most frequent loan amounts are $4,000, $15,000, $10,000, and $5,000.

The loan terms in this data set fall into three bins: 12, 36, or 60 months, with 36 months being the most common. Since there are only 3 discrete values, I am going to change the vector type from integer to a factor, so I can graph it categorically in the bivariate section. I would also find the length-of-loan variable easier to interpret as 1, 3, or 5 years.

Bivariate Summary

Loan term covaried with actual return. The return for one-year loans was mostly positive, but not much more than 0. Three-year loans brought in the highest return. Five-year loans were responsible for the most lost in return.

Credit score also varied with actual return. Credit scores centering right under 600 actually brought the highest level of return.

Otherwise, I really didn’t find any other patterns of covariation.

This plot shows that most loans that have a positive return on investment. The average return for a funded loan is 2.8 percent.

##   mean_return_percent median_return_percent
## 1             2.78961              12.62516

Prosper gives out loans that are one, three, or five years in duration. Almost 90 percent of loans are provided with a three year term. The median return for these loans is 13.6 percent. However, the average return is only 4 percent. Negative outliers are bringing the average lower than the median.

The median return for one-year loans is 5.9 percent, with average profit at 3.8 percent. And the median return for five-year loans is 5.8 percent, with average profit at negative 13.4 percent. As can be seen in the graph above, the second quartile for five-year loans falls into a negative return. The five-year loan category is not profitable for Prosper.
